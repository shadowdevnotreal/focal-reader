Focal Help

What is Focal?

Focal is an RSVP (Rapid Serial Visual Presentation) speed reading tool. Words appear one at a time in a fixed position so your eyes stay still, eliminating the scanning movement that slows traditional reading. This lets you read 2–3x faster with practice.

The highlighted letter is the ORP (Optimal Recognition Point) — the ideal fixation point for fastest word recognition.

The Science of RSVP

Traditional reading involves saccades — rapid eye movements that jump between fixation points. Each saccade takes 20–30ms, and your brain pauses 200–300ms at each fixation. With typical line-scanning, up to 30% of reading time is spent just moving your eyes.

RSVP eliminates saccades entirely by presenting words at a fixed location. Your eyes stay still while words stream past, letting your brain focus purely on comprehension. Research by Forster (1970) and Potter (1984) demonstrated that RSVP allows processing rates up to 700+ WPM for trained readers.

The Optimal Recognition Point (ORP) is based on research by Brysbaert & Nazir (1998) showing that fixating slightly left of center in a word yields the fastest recognition. For short words (1–5 letters), ORP is near position 2. For longer words, it shifts right but stays in the first third — where the most informative letters typically are.

Focal also uses intelligent pacing: longer words get slightly more display time, punctuation triggers natural pauses (like a period adding a half-beat), and paragraph breaks introduce longer pauses to simulate the mental reset of starting a new section.

Technology

Focal is a Progressive Web App (PWA) built as a single self-contained HTML file. It works offline after first load via a service worker cache. All your data — preferences, stats, bookmarks — stays in your browser using localStorage and IndexedDB. Nothing is sent to any server.

AI features are powered by Groq, which runs open-source LLMs (Llama 3.3 70B) at ultra-low latency. Your API key and text are sent directly to Groq — Focal never touches them.

File parsing uses PDF.js (Mozilla) for PDFs and JSZip for EPUBs, loaded on-demand from CDNs only when first needed.

Multi-language ORP adapts the recognition point for different scripts: Latin, Cyrillic, CJK (Chinese/Japanese/Korean), Arabic, and Devanagari each use script-appropriate fixation logic.

App Layout — Three Tabs

Read — Your reading surface. Load text, play/pause, adjust speed, and use the Summarize button. The gamification bar tracks your streak, daily goal, and milestone progress.

Learn — Active learning after reading. AI-powered quizzes (multiple choice, true/false, fill-in-the-blank), key points extraction, reading level analysis, and sentence diagramming.

Stats — Your library of saved texts, focus timer stats, and the full psychometric reading dashboard with charts, session log, and AI insights.

Getting Started

1. Paste text, upload a file, drag & drop, or fetch from a URL.

2. Press Play or Space to start.

3. Start at 200–250 WPM and gradually increase.

4. Use Chunk (1–3) to show multiple words at once.

5. Enable Ramp to start slow and build to target speed.

6. Switch to Learn to quiz yourself, then check Stats to track progress.

Your loaded text and position are auto-saved — refresh the page and everything is restored.

Zen Mode

Press Z to enter a distraction-free reading mode. The header fades, tabs hide, and only the word display and transport controls remain. Controls reappear on hover. Press Z or Esc to exit.

AI Features (Learn Tab)

Add your Groq API key in Settings to unlock AI-powered tools:

Summarize (Read tab) — Get a concise overview without leaving the reader.

Key Points — Extract the most important ideas as bullet points.

Quiz Me — Interactive quiz with multiple-choice, true/false, and fill-in-the-blank questions. Graded with explanations, scores saved per text.

Comprehension Check — Quick 4-question MC quiz focused on what you just read.

Explain Simply — Rewrite complex text in plain language.

Reading Level — Flesch-Kincaid grade analysis, vocabulary complexity, and recommended speed.

AI Insights (Stats tab) — Clinical-quality psychometric analysis of your reading patterns with personalized tips.

Reading Diagnostics

Focal tracks 6 psychometric metrics based on clinical reading assessment research:

Consistency (CV%) — How stable your reading speed is. Lower is better; clinical target <15%.

Avg Focus — Average sustained attention span (time between play and pause).

Regressions — Backward navigations per 1,000 words. High rates may indicate comprehension difficulty.

Stamina — WPM at end of session vs. start. 100% = no fatigue; <80% suggests shorter sessions.

Comprehension — Average quiz score across all interactive quizzes.

Effective WPM — Speed × comprehension rate. The gold-standard measure of actual reading efficiency.

Gamification

The progress bar on the Read tab shows your daily streak (consecutive days reading), daily goal progress (default 10 minutes), and milestone badges (100 → 500 → 1K → 5K → 10K → 25K → 50K → 100K → 250K words).

Focus Timer

Always visible in the header bar. Full Pomodoro with configurable work (5–60 min), short break (1–15 min), and long break (5–30 min) intervals. After a configurable number of rounds (2–8, default 4), you get the long break. Auto-start option for seamless cycling. Click the gear icon to configure. Reading auto-pauses when focus sessions end.

Touch Controls

Tap the display to play/pause. Swipe left/right to skip 5 words. Swipe up/down to adjust WPM by ±25.

Data & Privacy

Everything stays in your browser. Preferences, stats, bookmarks, quiz scores, and your loaded text are saved to localStorage. Library texts use IndexedDB. Nothing is sent to any server except AI prompts (sent directly to Groq when you use AI features).

Your session auto-saves — loaded text, reading position, and timer state all survive page refreshes. Use the refresh button in the header for a clean reload with zero data loss.

Granular clearing: In the Stats tab, you can independently clear the session log, daily activity, quiz scores, bookmarks, library, or timer stats. Factory Reset in Settings wipes everything.

Speed Reading Tips

Focus on the ORP. Let the highlighted letter anchor your gaze and allow surrounding characters to register peripherally.

Suppress subvocalization. To break past ~400 WPM, practice not "saying" words in your head. Try humming or counting while reading.

Use ramp-up. Start each session slower and accelerate gradually — this helps your brain warm up before hitting full speed.

Take breaks. Use the Focus Timer for 25-minute Pomodoro sessions. Comprehension drops after extended speed reading.

Test yourself. Switch to the Learn tab and take a quiz after reading. Your Effective WPM (speed × comprehension) is the metric that matters most.

Try Zen mode. Press Z for a distraction-free reading surface when you want total focus.
